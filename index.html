A bodhisattva is a central figure in Buddhism, especially in Mahayana Buddhism.

Core definition:
Someone who seeks full awakening (enlightenment) but chooses to delay their final liberation in order to help all other beings become free from suffering.

Breaking it down:
“Bodhi” = awakening or enlightenment
“Sattva” = being

So literally: “an awakening being”

Essential qualities:

  • Compassion
  • Wisdom
  • Altruistic intention

The key idea:
A bodhisattva makes a kind of inner commitment: “I will not fully exit suffering until all beings can be free.”

In the cleanest form: A bodhisattva is someone who orients their life around reducing suffering for all beings, while developing the clarity and capacity to do so effectively.
